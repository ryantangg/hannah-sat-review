The following text is from Walt Whitman's 1860 poem "Calamus 24."

I HEAR it is charged against me that I seek to destroy institutions;
But really I am neither for nor against institutions
(What indeed have I in common with them?—Or what with the destruction of them?),
Only I will establish in the Mannahatta [Manhattan] and in every city of These States, inland and seaboard,
And in the fields and woods, and above every keel [ship] little or large, that dents the water,
Without edifices, or rules, or trustees, or any argument,
The institution of the dear love of comrades.

Which choice best describes the overall structure of the text?

  • The speaker questions an increasingly prevalent attitude, then summarizes his worldview.
  • The speaker regrets his isolation from others, then predicts a profound change in society.
  • The speaker concedes his personal shortcomings, then boasts of his many achievements.
  • The speaker addresses a criticism leveled against him, then announces a grand ambition of his.
Why D is correct: The speaker begins by stating he has heard others accuse him of seeking to destroy institutions. He then addresses this criticism ("I am neither for nor against institutions") and announces his grand ambition: to establish "the institution of the dear love of comrades" everywhere in the country. The structure is: address criticism → announce ambition.
Why A is incorrect: While the speaker addresses an opinion of him that he believes to be untrue, he doesn't indicate this attitude has become increasingly prevalent. He also concludes by explaining his goal for the future, not summarizing his current worldview.

The expression 4x² + bx − 45, where b is a constant, can be rewritten as (hx + k)(x + j), where h, k, and j are integer constants. Which of the following must be an integer?
  • b/h
  • b/k
  • 45/h
  • 45/k
Solution:
Expand (hx + k)(x + j) = hx² + (jh + k)x + kj

Match to 4x² + bx − 45:
• h = 4 (leading coefficient)
• kj = −45 (constant term)
• Since j is an integer: j = −45/k
• For j to be an integer, −45/k must be an integer
• Therefore 45/k must be an integer → Answer D

Key technique: When a quadratic is factored, the constant term equals the product of the constants in each factor. Use this constraint to determine what must be an integer.

In the xy-plane, a parabola has vertex (9, −14) and intersects the x-axis at two points. If the equation of the parabola is written in the form y = ax² + bx + c, where a, b, and c are constants, which of the following could be the value of a + b + c?
  • −23
  • −19
  • −14
  • −12
Solution:
Vertex form: y = a(x − 9)² − 14
Expand: y = ax² − 18ax + 81a − 14

So: a = a, b = −18a, c = 81a − 14
a + b + c = a − 18a + 81a − 14 = 64a − 14

Since vertex is below x-axis and parabola crosses x-axis at two points → opens upward → a > 0

Test D: 64a − 14 = −12 → 64a = 2 → a = 1/32 > 0 ✓
Test C: 64a − 14 = −14 → a = 0 (not a parabola) ✗
Test B: 64a − 14 = −19 → a = −5/64 < 0 ✗
Test A: 64a − 14 = −23 → a = −9/64 < 0 ✗

Key insight: a + b + c = f(1) — it's the y-value when x = 1. Use vertex form, expand, and note that a must be positive for the parabola to open upward.

Function f is defined by f(x) = −ax + b, where a and b are constants. In the xy-plane, the graph of y = f(x) − 15 has a y-intercept at (0, −99/7). The product of a and b is 65/7. What is the value of a?
Solution:
y = f(x) − 15 = −ax + b − 15

Y-intercept means x = 0:
−99/7 = −a0 + b − 15 = −1 + b − 15 = b − 16
→ b = −99/7 + 16 = −99/7 + 112/7 = 13/7

Given ab = 65/7:
a(13/7) = 65/7
a = (65/7) ÷ (13/7) = 65/13 = 5

Key technique: When x = 0, any base a0 = 1. Use this to find b from the y-intercept, then use the product ab to find a.

In the given equation, p is a constant. The equation has no solution. What is the value of p?

−3x + 21px = x(−3 + 21p) = x(21p − 3) = 84

• If coefficient of x = 0 and the other side ≠ 0 → no solution

The equation x² + (y − 1)² = 49 represents circle A. Circle B is obtained by shifting circle A down 2 units in the xy-plane. Which of the following equations represents circle B?
  • (x − 2)² + (y − 1)² = 49
  • x² + (y − 3)² = 49
  • (x + 2)² + (y − 1)² = 49
  • x² + (y + 1)² = 49
Memorize da circle formula
Solution:
Circle formula: (x − h)² + (y − k)² = r² has center (h, k) and radius r.

Circle A: x² + (y − 1)² = 49 → center (0, 1), radius 7

Shift down 2 → subtract 2 from y-coordinate of center:
New center: (0, 1 − 2) = (0, −1)
Radius stays 7.

Circle B: x² + (y − (−1))² = 49 → x² + (y + 1)² = 49
Why B is incorrect: x² + (y − 3)² = 49 has center (0, 3), which is circle A shifted up 2 units, not down.

Shifting rules: Down means decrease k. (y − 1) shifted down 2 becomes (y − (−1)) = (y + 1), not (y − 3).
Q26Skipped | Correct: B (8 cm)
▼
Two identical rectangular prisms each have a height of 90 centimeters (cm). The base of each prism is a square, and the surface area of each prism is K cm². If the prisms are glued together along a square base, the resulting prism has a surface area of (92/47)K cm². What is the side length, in cm, of each square base?
  • 4
  • 8
  • 9
  • 16
Solution:
Let x = side length of the square base.

When glued together, two square faces are hidden:
Combined SA = 2K − 2x²

Set equal: 2K − 2x² = (92/47)K
→ 2K − (92/47)K = 2x²
→ (94/47 − 92/47)K = 2x²
→ (2/47)K = 2x² → K = 47x²

Each prism's SA: K = 2x² + 4(90x) = 2x² + 360x
So: 2x² + 360x = 47x²
→ 45x² − 360x = 0
→ x(45x − 360) = 0
→ x = 0 or x = 360/45 = 8

Key technique: When gluing prisms together, the combined SA = 2 × (individual SA) − 2 × (hidden face area). Set up the equation, then also express K in terms of x using the SA formula.
